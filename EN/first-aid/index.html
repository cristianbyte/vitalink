¿What are
first aid?

First aid is a set of basic care procedures and techniques provided to a person who has suffered a sudden injury or illness until professional medical assistance arrives or they are transported to a healthcare facility. The primary goal of first aid is to preserve life, prevent complications, alleviate pain and discomfort, and stabilize the affected person.

Why know
first aid?

Saving lives: Knowing first aid allows you to provide immediate assistance in emergency situations, which can make the difference between life and death for a person.

Reducing suffering: Providing first aid also involves alleviating the pain and discomfort of the affected person.

Critical time: Professional medical care may not be immediately available, so first aid provides the necessary help until medical assistance arrives.

What are
first aid for?

Preserving life: The primary goal of first aid is to keep the affected person alive.

Preventing complications: First aid can help prevent complications and further damage.

Relieving pain and discomfort: First aid may include measures to alleviate the pain and discomfort of the affected person.

Preventing the spread of infections: Risks of infection can be reduced by cleaning and covering wounds or burns.
